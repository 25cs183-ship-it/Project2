CSS, or Cascading Style Sheets, is a foundational language of the web used to control the visual presentation, formatting, and layout of documents written in markup languages like HTML. It handles design elements such as colors, fonts, spacing, positioning, and animations, transforming plain, unstyled text and structure into engaging and user-friendly web pages. The "cascading" aspect refers to the predictable hierarchy that determines which styles apply when multiple rules conflict, allowing styles to be defined once and reused across multiple pages, which dramatically improves efficiency, consistency, and maintenance of an entire website.

Butterflies are beautiful, winged insects belonging to the order Lepidoptera, known for their large, often brightly coloured, scaled wings and delicate, fluttering flight. They undergo a fascinating four-stage complete metamorphosis: egg, larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and adult. Adults possess a straw-like proboscis to sip nectar from flowers, playing a key role in pollination while feeding.
